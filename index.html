What is IELTS?


The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is designed to help you work, study or migrate to a country where English is the native language. This includes countries such as Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the UK and USA. IELTS is graded on a scale of 1-9.
IELTS is developed to provide a fair and accurate assessment of English language proficiency.
Test questions are developed by language specialists from Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the UK and the USA. The test covers four sections: Listening, Reading, Writing and Speaking.
More information can be found on IELTS official site.


Now I'll give you useful tips for IELTS preparation


Listening:

  1. Familiarise yourself with a range of accents.
  2. Don’t lose your concentration.
    To improve your concentration you need to practice active listening.
  3. Follow the instructions carefully.
    This especially applies when it comes to the word limit.
  4. Familiarise yourself with the different question types.
    Doing so will mean you’ll know exactly what to expect on test day and how to react to the question types you’re given.
  5. Practice listening to things only once.

Reading:

  1. Don’t expect to understand every word.
    If you don’t understand a word in the reading test there are two things you can do. You can look at the words and sentences around it for clues as to its meaning or you can move on and forget about it.
  2. Practice your reading skills NOT your IELTS skills.
    Reading for pleasure a little every day will not only improve your English skills but also improve your vocabulary. Practice makes perfect.
  3. Read the instructions VERY carefully.
  4. Timing is crucial.
  5. Ignore anything you already know about the topic.
    Please remember that you are being tested on your understanding of the text only; your own knowledge of the topic should not influence your answers.
  6. Practice slow and fast.
  7. Leave no blanks.
    Even if you don’t know the answer, make sure you attempt every question. You might get lucky and you have nothing to lose.

Writing:

  1. Don't spent more than 20 mins on task 1. You need 40 mins for task 2.
  2. Always write over 150 words for task 1 and over 250 words for task 2.
  3. You can plan your essay on the question paper. No extra paper is given for planning. The examiner will not look at your planning, only at your essay.
  4. Always organise your writing for both tasks into paragraphs.
  5. Check your spelling. You can use either American or British spelling.
  6. Keep all information focused and relevant. More does not mean better.

Speaking:

  1. Speak some English every day.
  2. Ask the examiner questions if you don’t understand.
  3. Do a 24-hour English warm up.
  4. Give full answers.
  5. Correct your mistakes.

Preparation

The biggest question - how to START prepairing? I'll say a simple but really useful thing - you need to set yourself a goal. And this goal should be formulated according to SMART Criteria. The SMART is an acronym that stands for Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, and Timely. For more information click on the picture below.



For example, my SMART goals:

  1. It was specific: I had to take the IELTS General.
  2. It was measurable: I needed to get at least 6.0 scores.
  3. It was achievable and realistic: I had basic knowledge in English by the start of preparation.
  4. It was time-limited: I set deadline January. I passed IELTS 4th of January.

It wasn't easy. But the result was worth it.
